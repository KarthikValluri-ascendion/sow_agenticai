# Statement of Work (SOW)
## Oasis Data Platform and AI Readiness Program

**Supplier:** Ascendion  
**Customer:** Oasis  
**Effective Date:** March 9, 2026  
**Initial Term:** March 9, 2026 to March 31, 2027

---

## 1. Project Summary and Strategic Engagement Framework

This Statement of Work establishes a strategic, high-touch managed services partnership between Ascendion and Oasis to modernize Oasis enterprise data architecture and enable scalable, AI-ready operations. The engagement extends beyond point delivery to include governance-led architecture planning, sustained platform reliability, and iterative optimization aligned with business priorities.

Ascendion will provide architecture and delivery services across data warehousing, transformation pipelines, orchestration, and quality controls under its delivery governance model. The parties acknowledge this program as an Alpha-circle strategic engagement with weekly governance visibility and structured checkpoints.

Before implementation work proceeds beyond agreed initiation checkpoints, Oasis and Ascendion will complete a formal **Data Architecture & Ethics Gate**. This intake gate includes review and sign-off of target-state architecture, source-to-target lineage considerations, data handling boundaries, and ethical AI guardrails.

---

## 2. Scope of Services

Ascendion will perform the following services:

1. Design and evolve the enterprise data architecture for Oasis, including Snowflake-aligned warehouse modeling and AI-ready data foundations.
2. Build and operationalize dbt-based transformation pipelines, with standardized testing, versioning, and deployment controls.
3. Establish and tune data quality, observability, and pipeline reliability practices for production operations.
4. Implement metadata, lineage-aware documentation, and governance artifacts to support auditability and controlled change management.
5. Execute **Architecture and Ethics Governance** through the **Data Architecture & Ethics Gate**, aligned with Ethical AI Review Board standards and required approvals at defined checkpoints.
6. Provide managed-services support for incident response, operational stabilization, and continuous improvement recommendations.

### 2.1 Out of Scope

Unless expressly added via written change request, this SOW excludes:

- Non-data application feature development outside pipeline and platform boundaries.
- Security or compliance controls owned exclusively by third-party systems not managed by Ascendion.
- New business domains or major platform migrations not described in the approved backlog and release plan.

---

## 3. Deliverables and Reporting

Ascendion will provide the following deliverables:

1. Data architecture blueprint and implementation roadmap.
2. Configured Snowflake objects and dbt models for prioritized domains.
3. Tested and documented data pipelines with runbook and operating procedures.
4. Gate artifacts and sign-off package for the **Data Architecture & Ethics Gate**.
5. **Weekly Status Reports** covering progress, risks, dependencies, and decisions.
6. **Weekly Client Sentiment Analysis** (or equivalent weekly satisfaction/sentiment insights) to satisfy ongoing Alpha reporting obligations.
7. Monthly governance summary with KPI trend snapshots and action tracking.

### 3.1 Compliance Gate (Mandatory Action)

The parties acknowledge that Project Managers must run **Automated Compliance Scanning** prior to the formal project kickoff meeting. Failure to complete the required scan may block the project from active status in the **DelEx portal** until remediated.

---

## 4. Timeline and Milestones

The engagement starts on the Effective Date and runs through the Initial Term.

1. **Phase 0 - Initiation and Gate Readiness (March 9, 2026 to March 20, 2026)**  
   Discovery, architecture baseline, ethics criteria alignment, and compliance prerequisites.
2. **Phase 1 - Data Architecture & Ethics Gate Completion (March 23, 2026 to April 10, 2026)**  
   Formal gate review, documented approvals, and controlled release plan.
3. **Phase 2 - Build and Stabilization Waves (April 13, 2026 to February 26, 2027)**  
   Incremental pipeline delivery, quality hardening, observability, and governance checkpoints.
4. **Phase 3 - Optimization and Transition Readiness (March 1, 2027 to March 31, 2027)**  
   Performance optimization, operational maturity review, and next-term planning.

Any material timeline change requires written approval through change control.

---

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

### 5.1 Ascendion Responsibilities

- Provide delivery leadership, architecture expertise, and managed-services execution capacity.
- Facilitate and document Data Architecture & Ethics Gate reviews and decisions.
- Deliver agreed artifacts, weekly reports, and operational metrics.
- Escalate risks, blockers, and compliance concerns in a timely manner.

### 5.2 Oasis Responsibilities

- Provide timely access to systems, data sources, SMEs, and required environments.
- Review and approve gate artifacts, architecture decisions, and milestone outcomes.
- Assign business and technical owners for issue resolution and prioritization.
- Participate in weekly governance and sentiment/status review cadences.

---

## 6. Governance and Change Control

The engagement will be governed through recurring weekly steering touchpoints, issue/risk logs, and decision records. Scope, timelines, dependencies, or assumptions that materially affect cost, schedule, or delivery obligations will be managed through a documented change request process requiring mutual written approval.

---

## 7. Acceptance Criteria

Deliverables will be considered accepted when:

1. The defined deliverable artifacts are submitted and reviewed.
2. Acceptance checks listed in the delivery plan are met.
3. Required Data Architecture & Ethics Gate approvals are recorded for relevant milestones.
4. No unresolved severity-1 compliance blockers remain for the applicable release.

---

## 8. Assumptions and Dependencies

- Oasis provides required access, data samples, and environment readiness within agreed windows.
- Third-party platform availability and licenses are maintained by the owning party.
- Compliance and security policies remain stable unless formally revised.
- Delays caused by missing dependencies may require timeline realignment via change control.
